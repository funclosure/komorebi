Field notes — what the research says

The phenomenon has a name木漏れ日

Komorebi names not the light itself but the relationship — leaves, light, and the movement between them. The implication: a static shadow texture is not the thing. The layer must breathe, or it's just a stain. A rule worth keeping: waiting things breathe, never freeze.

wabisabi-jp · thursd

Every dapple is a picture of the sun

Each gap in a canopy is a pinhole camera projecting the sun's disk — that's why dapples are round, and why they turn crescent during an eclipse. Softness grows with canopy height. So the 隙 door punches round light spots out of shadow rather than thresholding noise; it's physically honest and it reads instantly.

AAS eclipse projection · PetaPixel · Tufte, “Dappled light”

The stage word is gobo

Lighting designers shape light with a gobo — and tree-shadow gobos are currently everywhere as background textures (Figma sets, overlay loops). That's the risk: a baked overlay reads as stock. the shadow must be procedural and of the day — seeded, tinted by the day's own palette, never a PNG.

Figma gobo set · School of Motion

Wind is a sum of slow sines

The standard trick (GPU Gems): layer sines of different frequencies, let amplitude grow toward branch tips, add a whisper of high-frequency flutter for the leaves. This prototype does exactly that per branch segment — cheap enough for a 60fps backdrop, and directly portable to SwiftUI's Canvas or a Metal shader.

GPU Gems 3, ch. 6 · procedural tree pen

Where it lives in the code

Shadow generation math → a pure, seeded, unit-testable module (like color extraction), tint & compositing → the design-system layer — never inline in feature views. Seed it from the date (e.g. month×31+day) so the same day always wears the same tree.

What the controls probe

The open questions, made draggable: 日色 probes whether the shadow competes with the app's own day-color atmosphere or rides inside it; 空 is the empty-day answer (procedural, always available); the content toggle asks whether light falls on the whole wall or only behind the text.

Prototype only — three doors, one paper. Next step: pick a door, then port the winner as a seeded generator behind a native canvas compositor (SwiftUI Canvas, Jetpack Compose, or plain layers). The extraction door — lifting leaf silhouettes from the user's own photos — stays open as a second source feeding the same compositor.
